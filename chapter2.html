Signs of Trigonometric Functions (ASTC)

"Trigonometric functions have positive or negative values depending on the quadrant in which the angle θ lies."

Quadrant I (0 < θ < π/2) ALL Functions +ve
Quadrant II (π/2 < θ < π) Only sin θ, cosec θ are +ve Others -ve
Quadrant III (π < θ < 3π/2) Only tan θ, cot θ are +ve Others -ve
Quadrant IV (3π/2 < θ < 2π) Only cos θ, sec θ are +ve Others -ve

Domain, Range & Periodicity

sin θ Domain: All Reals | Range: [-1, 1] | Period: 2π
cos θ Domain: All Reals | Range: [-1, 1] | Period: 2π
tan θ Domain: Reals except odd multiples of π/2 | Range: All Reals | Period: π
cosec θ Domain: Reals except integer multiples of π | Range: Reals outside (-1,1) | Period: 2π
sec θ Domain: Reals except odd multiples of π/2 | Range: Reals outside (-1,1) | Period: 2π
cot θ Domain: Reals except integer multiples of π | Range: All Reals | Period: π

Negative Angle Properties

💡 Tip: cos and sec absorb negativity. They act like "positive people"!

sin(-θ) = -sin θ
cos(-θ) = cos θ
tan(-θ) = -tan θ
cosec(-θ) = -cosec θ
sec(-θ) = sec θ
cot(-θ) = -cot θ

Polar Coordinate Systems

• Cartesian Point conversion: (x, y) = (r * cos θ, r * sin θ)

• Radius r = √(x² + y²)

• Direction Angle θ = tan⁻¹(y / x)
